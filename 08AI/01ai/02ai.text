To learn AI (Artificial Intelligence) and ML (Machine Learning) effectively, it's important to follow a structured approach, as both fields are vast and have several specialized areas. Below is a comprehensive list of key topics to guide your learning journey:

1. Foundation Topics
Mathematics and Statistics for AI & ML
Linear Algebra:
Vectors, matrices, and operations
Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues
Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)
Calculus:
Differentiation and integration
Partial derivatives
Gradient descent
Probability and Statistics:
Probability theory and Bayes' theorem
Distributions (Gaussian, Binomial, Poisson)
Hypothesis testing
Statistical inference
Expectation, variance, and covariance
Optimization:
Convex optimization
Gradient descent, stochastic gradient descent
Loss functions (MSE, cross-entropy)

Language models (GPT, BERT)

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

Learning Path Overview
Start with foundational mathematics (linear algebra, statistics, and calculus).
Learn basic programming (preferably Python).
Learn core machine learning concepts like supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.
Deep dive into deep learning and neural networks, focusing on frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch.
Explore specialized AI fields like NLP and computer vision.
Apply your knowledge to real-world projects and begin learning deployment techniques.
By following these topics step by step, you will develop a solid understanding of both AI and Machine Learning. Each area builds upon previous knowledge, so taking your time with the fundamentals will pay off as you progress into advanced topics.
